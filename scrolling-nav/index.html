About Me


I'm a Junior at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill majoring in Psychology and Economics, with a minor in Anthropology. I am from Beijing, China. I previously studied abroad at The Chinese University of Hong Kong as an exchange student. On campus, I'm an active member of the UNC Glee Club and the UNC Squash Club. Outside of class, I work in psychology research lab at UNC, where I explore my passion for understanding human behavior, particularly in areas like consumer behavior, industrial psychology, and decision-making. I aspire to pursue a PhD in this field in the future. My mission is to uncover the mechanisms behind human behavior and use that knowledge to create meaningful, real-world impact.

Six Experiences/Ideas That Have Shaped My Perspective

  1. Watching Blue Planet from BBC: appreciation for the natural world and think more critically about environmental sustainability
  2. TED Talk, Grit: The Power of Passion and Perseverance by Angela Lee Duckworth: influencing me how to approach challenges in both academics and life
  3. The discovery of neuroplasticity: Learning how the brain can rewire itself has reinforced my belief in the power of growth and adaptability.
  4. A personal travel experience in Puerto Rico: Immersing myself in the island's vibrant culture and natural beauty
  5. Steve Jobs's Stanford Commencement Speech: His message about connecting the dots, love, and loss has stayed with me, reminding me to trust the journey and pursue what I truly love
  6. Volunteering with children with disabilities and elderly individuals: taught me empathy, patience, and the value of creating inclusive communities
